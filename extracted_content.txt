Key challenges in the current Partner onboarding
1. Inadequate Support for Large Fleet Management
:
Current State
: 80% of EV supply and 40-50% of E-Loader supply is through 3PLs, which will likely increase.3 PL supply has grown by ~3.5 X in 2023-2024 and the trend is likely to continue with Projected MOM growth of 12-15%.In next 2-3 years we are expecting 25-30% our supply will come through 3PL vendors. Currently, In EV categories if we are onboarding 100 vehicles in a month, we receive almost 200-300 requests (2X to 3X of onboarding) to change drivers on a vehicle. Our current systems are optimised for Driver-Owner personas, leading to inefficiencies in managing large fleets where drivers frequently change vehicles. This results in poor experience, revenue loss, low retention, and increased fraud risk.
Key Issues
:
Driver Change Process
: Managing frequent driver changes is cumbersome, with high TAT (1-2 days) and manual workarounds. This causes significant earning loss (e.g., For LCV in July we received 1249 requests for driver change with  1 day TAT and assuming an average earning of Rs 1000 it would have led to ‚Çπ12.49 lakhs loss for LCV vendors).
Incomplete driver documentation leads to difficulties in tracking fraudulent activities, as a workaround teams change only the phone numbers of the driver rather than changing all details of the driver or re-onboarding it, which also leads to customers seeing wrong driver information on app
Penalties Misaligned
: SLA penalties are tied to vehicles instead of individual drivers, leading to unfair suspensions and keeping the vehicle operative even if the driver is changed during suspension
2. Ability to Launch Partner onboarding in  New Countries
:
Current State
: Currently in the international markets we onboard partners via Backend office teams and it doesn‚Äôt have a provision in the partner app to onboard via app directly, which creates a hindrance in growing supply organically or doing any marketing activities and increases the cost of onboarding. While when the current scale is low teams are able to manage it, however as we grow, lack of this capability can hinder the supply growth and increase the cost of acquisition significantly, along with that it also hampers the brand perception negatively. Currently we onboard Approx 800-900 partners in a month in Bangladesh, this number will go upto 1500 per month by March.In UAE we onboard 250-300 in a month and are yet to start in turkey
Lack of in-app onboarding for international markets forces reliance on FOS channels, increasing acquisition costs and limiting scalability. Expanding app-based onboarding is crucial for growth.
Impact
: Delayed market entry and inefficient scaling of partner acquisition.
3. System Loopholes Enabling Fraud
:
Current State
: The system allows multiple users to register with the same documents creating opportunities for fraud. Along with that there is opportunity to improve the visibility of OCR and document verification API response related information for the SCMS team so that the fraud can be curbed
Recent Incident
:
In ~14000 Orders team flagged the Geo spoofing issue in H1, in majority of these cases partners created multiple driver profiles.
Doc link

Partners upload the edited/ forged documents with edited details like Number, Photo, Name etc
4. Compliance challenges:
Currently, we store the Aadhar number and images in our database which is not a complaint approach as per the govt, ideally we should store it in the Aadhar vault
Also, these documents are easily accessible
5. High Drop-off Rates in the Onboarding Journey
:
Context:
About 77% of leads drop off between the start of onboarding (owner details page load) and the training start. Specifically, ~50% drop off before submitting owner details, and ~20% drop off at the vehicle details stage.
Dashboard link
.
Owner Details Page
: Approximately 30% of users who drop off are not potential partners (20% are customers, 9% are existing partners). Another 35% drop off due to unavailable documents, while 19% leave the process because they are busy at that moment.
Research doc link
.
Vehicle Details Page
: Analysis shows a ~20% drop-off rate on the vehicle details page, with 26% of these users leaving due to RC-related issues.
Document link
for more details and in dept analysis
Document Management
: Analysis suggests In 63% of rejected cases, the problem was image quality (15% photocopies, 21% blurred images, 8% cropped images). Additionally, 17% of users uploaded someone else‚Äôs documents, and 10% submitted the same side of a document twice. These insights will inform improvements to the document upload experience.

Current State

Performance
: P75 TAT for onboarding is 2 days for 3W, 9 days for 7Ft, and 6 days for 8ft categories.

7. Tech challenges:
High Lead Time for Changes
:
Underlying Issues
:
Tightly Coupled Systems
: Our architecture is rigid, with highly intertwined systems that slow down any modifications.
Shared Databases
: Multiple teams and services depend on shared databases, leading to bottlenecks and coordination issues.
Architectural Limitations
: The current design constraints make it difficult to implement changes swiftly.(Independent Rollouts and scaling, Multiple domains bundled up and others)
Complex Data Relationships
: Data definitions and relationships are complex, making even small changes time-consuming and error-prone.
Inflexibility in Managing Core Functions
:
Key Problems
:
Role and Relationship Changes
: Changes to roles (e.g., driver-owner, driver-vehicle relationships) are not seamlessly managed, requiring workaround solutions like de-boarding entities.
Increasing Code Complexity
:
Challenges
:
High Cognitive Load
: The complexity of the codebase has grown, increasing the difficulty for developers to work efficiently.
Technical Debt
: Accumulated tech debt makes it harder to maintain and improve the system.
High Bug Susceptibility
:
Problem
: The system's complexity and technical debt have made it more prone to bugs, impacting reliability and user experience.
